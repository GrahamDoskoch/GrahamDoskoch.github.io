About Me

I'm a freshman and prospective astrophysics and mathematics major at Swarthmore College. My interests are in stellar astrophysics and stellar evolution, including the final stages of the lives of massive stars. I'm currently working in the SwARCC research group, Swarthmore's arm of the Arecibo Remote Command Center (ARCC) project. ARCC looks at data gathered from the Arecibo Observatory and the Green Bank Telescope to identify possible pulsars. Some of this data is used by the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves (NANOGRAV). NANOGRAV is putting together a pulsar timing array, one of only a few in the world.

Outside physics, I'm a runner on the Swarthmore cross country and track and field teams. I'm also an avid music fan; I listen to several British folk groups (Steeleye Span, Fairport Convenction, and Silly Wizard), as well as various rock and alternative bands (Dire Straits, Crowded House, Belle and Sebastian, Talking Heads, and others).
