Research

Rotating radio transients
Currently, I'm studying a group of rotating radio transients (RRATs) discovered by the PALFA survey at the Arecibo Observatory. RRATs behave like erratic pulsars, and usually need to be studied through single-pulse analyses instead of typical methods of observing and timing pulsars. They're still not very well understood, and may provide clues as to the behavior of older pulsars whose emission mechanisms may be failing.

NANOGrav
I'm an associate member of the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves, or NANOGrav, a pulsar timing array designed to detect gravitational waves in the nanohertz regime. This opens up a new region of the gravitational wave universe, allowing us to study supermassive black hole binaries and the hypothesized stochastic gravitational wave background.
